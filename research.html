The Science of Movement

Evidence-based insights into how physical activity transforms your work life.

Boosting Cognitive Function

Studies from Stanford University show that walking increases creative output by an average of 60%. The act of movement stimulates the release of neurotransmitters that enhance focus and memory retention.

Ergonomics & Longevity

Research published in the British Journal of Sports Medicine indicates that breaking up sedentary time with "activity snacks" significantly reduces metabolic risks. Even 30 seconds of stretching resets the musculoskeletal system.

Emotional Wellbeing

Physical activity is directly linked to lower cortisol levels. A supportive work environment where movement is encouraged leads to higher job satisfaction and lower burnout rates.

Cardiac Health

The Lancet published findings that even light-intensity physical activity is associated with a 14% reduced risk of all-cause mortality. Standing instead of sitting burns approximately 50 extra calories per hour.
